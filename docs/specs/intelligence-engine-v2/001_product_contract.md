# SPEC 001 — Product Contract

This is the anti-chaos document. Every feature, prompt, and validation rule must be derivable
from this contract. If it cannot be derived, the feature is out of scope.

---

## User

**Who:** A B2B SaaS or services founder, typically CEO or co-founder, at a company between
£500k–£10m ARR. They are probably the primary salesperson. They have not yet built a repeatable
GTM system. They have strong product conviction and weak distribution clarity.

**Context when they receive the output:** They are opening a physical letter. They did not request
the analysis. It arrived unsolicited. They have approximately 90 seconds before they decide to
read further or discard it.

---

## Use Case

The system produces a single short diagnostic document, formatted for physical mail delivery.
The document is sent as the opening move in a cold outreach sequence — not as a deliverable to
a paying client.

The report must earn a reply. Not a download. Not a form submission. A reply.

---

## Report Reaction Goal

The founder should finish reading and think one of these things:

> "How do they know this about us?"

> "We've been trying to figure this out for six months."

> "I need to talk to whoever wrote this."

The report must never produce:

> "This is a nice summary of our website."

> "This is generic advice."

> "This is an investor pitch deck in letter format."

---

## Core Decision Changed

The report must change one specific decision the founder is currently making or deferring.

That decision is always one of:
- how they are currently reaching their next buyer
- how they are currently explaining what they sell
- how they are structuring the next 6 months of growth effort

The report does NOT change product decisions, team decisions, or fundraising decisions.

---

## Output Structure

The output is exactly three sections:

1. **The Diagnosis** — states the structural GTM condition the company is in. No hedging.
   Says what is actually happening, not what might be happening.

2. **Why This Happens** — explains the 2–3 structural forces that produce the diagnosis.
   Each force is causal, not descriptive. Shows why the condition persists.

3. **The Opportunity** — names the single most agency-deliverable lever. Ties directly back
   to the diagnosis. Shows what changes and why it is the right sequence.

**Format:**
- Prose only. No bullet lists. No headers beyond the three section titles.
- Maximum 900 words total (≤3 pages at standard business letter formatting).
- Printed on company letterhead. Sent by post.

---

## Non-Goals

- **Not investor diligence.** Do not frame findings in terms of market size, TAM, or investor risk.
- **Not generic company research.** Every sentence must be specific to this company.
- **Not broad market intelligence.** No industry benchmarks, no competitor comparisons.
- **Not product strategy.** Do not recommend product changes, features, or roadmap shifts.
- **Not a comprehensive audit.** The system produces one diagnosis, not a ranked list of problems.
- **Not a deliverable to a paying client.** The report is a cold outreach artifact. It must earn
  the next conversation, not try to deliver all the value upfront.
- **Not benchmarking** in v1. No relative scoring against other companies.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

Any team member who reads this spec must be able to answer:

1. **Who is this for?**
   A B2B founder who has not requested analysis, opening a physical letter.

2. **What action should it cause?**
   The founder replies. Or the founder forwards it internally. Either is a win.

3. **What should the report never become?**
   - A consulting memo
   - A summary of their own website
   - Generic GTM advice
   - A list of observations without a single structural diagnosis

4. **What is the one structural decision it targets?**
   How this founder reaches their next buyer.

5. **How long is it?**
   ≤900 words. ≤3 printed pages. Three sections. Prose only.
