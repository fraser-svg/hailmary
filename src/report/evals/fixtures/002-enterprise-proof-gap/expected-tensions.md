# Expected Tensions — Fixture 002: Enterprise Proof Gap

## Must Detect

These tensions must be identified for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Enterprise positioning vs customer reality

- Type: enterprise_positioning_vs_customer_reality
- Severity: high
- The company consistently positions itself as an enterprise platform, but all observable customer evidence — case studies, reviews, testimonials — involves small organizations (5-15 employees). No enterprise customers are identifiable.
- Signal pairs: enterprise narrative signal + customer base signal + lack of enterprise proof signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_004, ev_005, ev_006, ev_011

### 2. Ambition vs proof

- Type: ambition_vs_proof
- Severity: high
- The company's enterprise ambition is visible in marketing, press, and product feature emphasis. However, observable evidence does not demonstrate enterprise adoption, enterprise deal sizes, or enterprise-scale deployments. Ambition outpaces demonstrated capability.
- Signal pairs: enterprise positioning signal + pricing signal + hiring signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_003, ev_008, ev_010

## Nice to Detect

### 3. Narrative scale vs operational evidence

- Type: narrative_scale_vs_operational_evidence
- Severity: medium
- Marketing language speaks to "the world's leading companies" and "enterprise scale," but the operational signals — SMB sales team, high-volume CS portfolios, low price points — are structured for small organizations.
- Signal pairs: enterprise narrative signal + hiring signal + pricing signal

### 4. Positioning vs market fit

- Type: positioning_vs_market_fit
- Severity: medium
- The company positions against legacy enterprise tools but customers describe the product's value in SMB terms: quick setup, simplicity, no IT department needed. The positioning targets enterprise; the product-market fit evidence points to SMB.
- Signal pairs: customer language signal + enterprise positioning signal

## Must Avoid

### Tensions invented without signal support

- Tensions must arise from signal pairs grounded in evidence. Speculative tensions about enterprise readiness without supporting signals are invalid.

### Causal or explanatory statements

- "The company fails at enterprise because its product is too simple." This is a hypothesis, not a tension. Tensions identify structural strain between signals — they do not explain causes.

### Trivial tensions

- "Company has both small and large customers." Unless the tension is between positioning and evidence, this is not a meaningful structural contradiction.

### Tone-based tensions

- "The marketing sounds too aggressive." Unless tied to evidence of misalignment, tone alone is not a tension.
