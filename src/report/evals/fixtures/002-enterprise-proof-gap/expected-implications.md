# Expected Implications — Fixture 002: Enterprise Proof Gap

## Must Detect

All implications must remain conditional — they describe consequences if the hypotheses are true, not assertions of fact.

### 1. Enterprise credibility may lag enterprise ambition

If the enterprise positioning is aspirational, enterprise buyers conducting due diligence may find the absence of enterprise references, case studies, and large-scale deployments to be a credibility gap. The more aggressive the enterprise narrative, the more conspicuous the lack of enterprise proof becomes.

Conditional on: hypothesis that enterprise positioning is aspirational

### 2. Larger customers may require capabilities not yet demonstrated

If the current customer base is primarily 5-15 person organizations, the product's fitness for 500+ person enterprises is unproven. Enterprise requirements — advanced permissioning, complex workflow orchestration, multi-team governance, integration depth — may exceed what the product has demonstrated in practice.

Conditional on: hypothesis that SMB traction does not prove enterprise readiness

### 3. Positioning risk may increase as enterprise buyers scrutinize proof

If the company actively pursues enterprise deals, prospects will seek reference customers of similar size and industry. The gap between "enterprise-grade" marketing and SMB customer references may create sales cycle friction, extended evaluation periods, or lost deals.

Conditional on: hypothesis that enterprise positioning outpaces enterprise proof

### 4. SMB customer base may be underserved by enterprise messaging

If the actual customer base is SMB, the enterprise-heavy marketing may be creating a positioning mismatch for the company's best customers. SMB buyers may feel the product is "not for them" or may have different expectations about support, pricing, and complexity based on the enterprise framing.

Conditional on: hypothesis that the company builds traction in smaller organizations

## Nice to Detect

### 5. Sales motion may require adjustment if enterprise proof remains thin

If the company attempts to move upmarket without building enterprise proof, the SMB-oriented sales team (2-4 week cycles, $5K-$25K deals) may not be equipped for enterprise sales cycles that typically span 3-9 months with multiple stakeholders.

### 6. Investor expectations may be calibrated to enterprise narrative

If the Series A was raised on an enterprise thesis, investors may expect enterprise metrics — net revenue retention above 120%, expansion revenue, large logo acquisition. If the company's actual trajectory is SMB, there may be a future disconnect between investor expectations and operational reality.

## Must Avoid

### Direct strategic recommendations

- "StratusFlow should reposition as an SMB tool." The implication stage identifies consequences, not prescriptions.

### Deterministic predictions

- "StratusFlow will fail to close enterprise deals." Implications must remain conditional on hypothesis validity.

### Moral judgments

- "This positioning is misleading and will damage trust." The engine analyzes strategic dynamics, not ethical conduct.

### Predictions with timeframes

- "Within 12 months, the company will need to pivot." The engine does not predict timelines.

### Unconditional assertions

- "Enterprise customers will reject StratusFlow." Implications must be framed as conditional consequences, not certainties.
