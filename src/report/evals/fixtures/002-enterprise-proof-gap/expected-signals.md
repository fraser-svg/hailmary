# Expected Signals — Fixture 002: Enterprise Proof Gap

## Must Detect

These signals must be extracted for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Enterprise narrative stronger than enterprise customer evidence

- Category: positioning
- Inference: light_inference
- The company's marketing repeatedly uses "enterprise" as a positioning qualifier, but no observable customer references, case studies, or testimonials involve enterprise-scale organizations.
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_004, ev_005

### 2. Customer base skewed toward small organizations

- Category: customer
- Inference: direct
- All identifiable customers are small: a 12-person agency, an 8-person startup, a 5-person team, a 15-person consulting firm, and a 10-person SaaS startup. No customer with more than 20 employees is observable.
- Key evidence: ev_004, ev_005, ev_006, ev_007, ev_011

### 3. Pricing tiers align with SMB or mid-market adoption

- Category: pricing
- Inference: direct
- Starter tier starts at $49/user with a 10-user cap. Enterprise tier at $199/user is priced competitively with mid-market tools. Per-user pricing with low entry points suggests self-serve SMB adoption, not enterprise procurement.
- Key evidence: ev_003

### 4. Hiring signals emphasize SMB sales motion

- Category: talent
- Inference: direct
- Sales roles explicitly target SMB deal sizes ($5K-$25K ARR) with 2-4 week cycles. Customer success roles manage 80-120 account portfolios. No enterprise AE, strategic account, or field sales roles observed.
- Key evidence: ev_008, ev_009

## Nice to Detect

These signals add depth but are not required for passing.

### 5. Enterprise positioning language repeated heavily in marketing

- Category: positioning
- Inference: direct
- The word "enterprise" appears prominently in homepage copy, product features, press coverage, and content hooks. The repetition exceeds what would be expected from organic enterprise adoption.
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_010

### 6. Lack of enterprise proof signals

- Category: credibility
- Inference: light_inference
- No enterprise logos, no large-scale deployment references, no case studies involving companies with more than 20 employees. The absence of enterprise proof is notable given the strength of enterprise positioning.
- Key evidence: ev_004, ev_005, ev_006, ev_011

### 7. Customers compare product to SMB tools, not enterprise platforms

- Category: customer
- Inference: direct
- Reviewers compare StratusFlow to Zapier and Make (SMB automation tools) and contrast it favorably against ServiceNow (enterprise tool) as "overkill." Customers perceive the product in an SMB competitive frame.
- Key evidence: ev_006, ev_011

## Must Avoid

The signal extraction stage must NOT produce these.

### Claims that the company cannot serve enterprise customers

- "StratusFlow is not an enterprise product."
- This is a conclusion, not an observation. The dossier shows the company lacks enterprise proof, not that it lacks enterprise capability.

### Accusations of dishonesty

- "The company is misleading customers about its enterprise capabilities."
- Signals must remain observational. Positioning-reality gaps are common and may be intentional strategy.

### Causal explanations

- "StratusFlow targets SMB because it cannot compete at enterprise scale."
- This is a hypothesis, not a signal. Signals observe; they do not explain why.

### Restatements of dossier facts

- "The company offers a workflow automation platform."
- This is a fact summary, not an analytical observation.
