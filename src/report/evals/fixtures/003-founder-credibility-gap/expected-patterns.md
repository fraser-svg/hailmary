# Expected Patterns — Fixture 003: Founder Credibility Gap

## Must Detect

### 1. Credibility concentrated in founder identity

Multiple tensions converge on the same structural form: the company's credibility, customer value delivery, thought leadership, and public narrative are all anchored to the founder personally rather than distributed across an institutional structure.

This is not a single observation. It is a recurring pattern across:

- founder credibility vs institutional depth (founder_credibility_vs_institutional_depth tension)
- narrative authority vs operational scale (narrative_authority_vs_operational_scale tension)
- personal brand vs company identity (personal_brand_vs_company_identity tension)

The pattern appears in every observable dimension:

- marketing references the founder by name and credentials
- press coverage frames the company as the founder's personal project
- customers attribute value to the founder's direct involvement
- thought leadership is exclusively founder-generated
- no other institutional voice or authority is visible

Constituent tensions: founder_credibility_vs_institutional_depth, narrative_authority_vs_operational_scale

### 2. Institutional leadership depth lagging company ambition

The company's positioning implies organizational maturity — "redefining decision-making for modern organizations" — but the observable leadership structure consists of one executive and junior support staff. The gap between institutional ambition and institutional depth is visible across:

- team page listing only the founder as a leader
- all hires at junior individual contributor level
- no senior leadership roles being recruited
- founder personally performing functions that scaling companies typically distribute across multiple executives

Constituent tensions: narrative_authority_vs_operational_scale, leadership_concentration_vs_scaling_needs

## Nice to Detect

### 3. Founder-centric growth structure

The company may be operating a growth model where the founder's personal brand and direct involvement serve as the primary customer acquisition and retention mechanism. This structure — founder as sales, marketing, customer success, and product evangelist simultaneously — is a recognizable early-stage pattern that the evidence supports.

## Must Avoid

### Single-tension restatement

A pattern is not a restatement of one tension. If the system produces "the founder is too involved" as a pattern, it has failed to synthesize across multiple tensions and domains.

### Causal explanations about founder motivations

"The founder centralizes authority because the company is too early to hire senior leaders." Patterns observe structural form — they do not explain founder decision-making or attribute intent.

### Industry-level patterns

"Many early-stage startups are founder-dependent" is an industry observation, not a company-specific pattern derived from this dossier's signals and tensions.
