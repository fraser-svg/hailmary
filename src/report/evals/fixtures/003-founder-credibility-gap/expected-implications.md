# Expected Implications — Fixture 003: Founder Credibility Gap

## Must Detect

All implications must remain conditional — they describe consequences if the hypotheses are true, not assertions of fact.

### 1. Scaling may require distributing credibility beyond the founder

If the company's credibility is currently anchored to the founder personally, growth beyond the founder's personal bandwidth may require building institutional credibility signals — a visible leadership team, team-led content, and customer relationships managed by people other than the founder.

Conditional on: hypothesis that company credibility depends heavily on founder authority

### 2. Enterprise buyers may seek institutional authority signals

If the company pursues larger customers, enterprise procurement processes may evaluate institutional maturity — executive team depth, organizational resilience, and continuity risk. A company whose public identity is inseparable from a single individual may face additional scrutiny from enterprise buyers assessing vendor risk.

Conditional on: hypothesis that credibility is concentrated in the founder

### 3. Leadership depth may become increasingly important as the company grows

If the current operating model — founder as sales, customer success, content creator, and product evangelist — continues without delegation, the company's capacity to serve additional customers may be constrained by the founder's available time and energy.

Conditional on: hypothesis that institutional leadership structures are still emerging

### 4. Founder bandwidth may influence deal flow and partnerships

If the founder is personally involved in every customer implementation, demo, and relationship, the number of concurrent deals and customer relationships may be limited by one person's capacity. This may become visible in sales cycle length, response times, or customer attention quality as demand grows.

Conditional on: hypothesis that founder involvement is central to customer value

## Nice to Detect

### 5. Investor perception may evolve as leadership structure matures

If the seed round was raised on the strength of the founder's personal credibility and McKinsey pedigree, future investors may evaluate whether the company has developed institutional capability beyond the founder. Series A investors typically assess team depth as a scaling indicator.

## Must Avoid

### Direct strategic recommendations

- "CatalystIQ should hire a CTO and VP Sales immediately." The implication stage identifies consequences, not prescriptions.

### Deterministic predictions about company outcomes

- "The company will hit a scaling wall within 12 months." Implications must remain conditional on hypothesis validity.

### Moral judgments

- "It is irresponsible to run a company this way." The engine analyzes strategic dynamics, not management conduct.

### Predictions with timeframes

- "Within two funding rounds, the founder will need to step back." The engine does not predict timelines.

### Unconditional assertions

- "Enterprise customers will reject a founder-dependent company." Implications must be framed as conditional consequences, not certainties.
