# Expected Signals — Fixture 003: Founder Credibility Gap

## Must Detect

These signals must be extracted for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Founder visibility dominates company narrative

- Category: positioning
- Inference: direct
- The founder is prominently featured across all external-facing content: homepage, blog, webinars, press coverage, and conference appearances. All 22 blog posts and all 14 webinars are authored or presented by the founder exclusively. Marketing language repeatedly references the founder by name and her McKinsey credentials.
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_003, ev_011

### 2. Founder appears central to customer relationships

- Category: customer
- Inference: direct
- Customers describe the founder as their primary point of contact during evaluation, setup, and ongoing engagement. Case study and reviews reference the founder by name and attribute value to her personal involvement rather than to a team or product capability.
- Key evidence: ev_007, ev_008, ev_009

### 3. Leadership team appears thin beyond the founder

- Category: talent
- Inference: direct
- The team page lists the founder as the only executive. Four team members hold individual contributor titles (Data Engineer, Full-Stack Developer, Product Designer, Operations Coordinator). No CTO, VP Engineering, VP Product, Head of Sales, or COO is listed or being recruited.
- Key evidence: ev_002, ev_006

### 4. Hiring signals emphasize junior operational roles

- Category: talent
- Inference: direct
- All 5 open positions are junior individual contributor roles reporting directly to the CEO. Roles include data analyst, junior developers, operations associate, and marketing coordinator. No senior leadership, management, or executive-level positions are being recruited.
- Key evidence: ev_006, ev_010

## Nice to Detect

These signals add depth but are not required for passing.

### 5. Thought leadership concentrated in founder voice

- Category: positioning
- Inference: direct
- All published content — 22 blog posts, 14 webinars, weekly live demos — is authored or presented by the founder. No guest contributors, team authors, or other company voices appear in any published content. The company's thought leadership is exclusively the founder's voice.
- Key evidence: ev_003, ev_011

### 6. Press coverage frames company around founder persona

- Category: credibility
- Inference: light_inference
- Forbes profile describes the founder as "the driving force" and frames CatalystIQ as "a vehicle for Kapoor's vision." TechCrunch introduces her as "the visionary behind CatalystIQ." No other team members are quoted or referenced in any press coverage. The company's media identity is the founder's personal identity.
- Key evidence: ev_004, ev_005

## Must Avoid

The signal extraction stage must NOT produce these.

### Accusations of poor management

- "The founder is failing to build a leadership team."
- This is a judgment, not an observation. The dossier shows limited leadership depth — it does not demonstrate management failure.

### Causal explanations about company success or failure

- "CatalystIQ succeeds because of the founder's personal involvement."
- This is a hypothesis, not a signal. Signals observe patterns; they do not attribute causation.

### Statements implying founder-led companies are inherently weak

- "This company structure is unsustainable."
- Founder-led operating models are common in early-stage companies and may be intentional. Signals must remain observational without structural value judgments.

### Restatements of dossier facts

- "The company sells a business intelligence platform."
- This is a fact summary, not an analytical observation.
