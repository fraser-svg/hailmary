# Expected Tensions — Fixture 003: Founder Credibility Gap

## Must Detect

These tensions must be identified for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Founder credibility vs institutional depth

- Type: founder_credibility_vs_institutional_depth
- Severity: high
- The company's credibility, customer relationships, and public narrative are concentrated in the founder's personal authority and expertise. However, the observable leadership structure — a single executive with junior individual contributors — does not demonstrate institutional depth that could sustain this credibility independently of the founder.
- Signal pairs: founder visibility signal + leadership depth signal + hiring signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_006, ev_007, ev_008

### 2. Narrative authority vs operational scale

- Type: narrative_authority_vs_operational_scale
- Severity: high
- The company positions itself as "redefining decision-making for modern organizations" — an institutional ambition — but the operational evidence shows a founder personally handling sales, customer onboarding, content creation, and product demos. The narrative implies organizational scale; the operations imply founder-as-organization.
- Signal pairs: founder visibility signal + customer relationship signal + hiring signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_003, ev_007, ev_008, ev_009, ev_011

## Nice to Detect

### 3. Personal brand vs company identity

- Type: personal_brand_vs_company_identity
- Severity: medium
- Press coverage, marketing, and customer references consistently frame CatalystIQ through the founder's personal identity rather than as an independent institution. The company's brand and the founder's personal brand appear to be nearly indistinguishable.
- Signal pairs: press coverage signal + founder visibility signal + thought leadership signal

### 4. Leadership concentration vs scaling needs

- Type: leadership_concentration_vs_scaling_needs
- Severity: medium
- The founder appears to fill multiple critical roles simultaneously — CEO, sales lead, customer success, content creator, and product evangelist. As the company grows, these functions typically require dedicated leadership. No delegation of these roles is observable.
- Signal pairs: customer relationship signal + hiring signal + leadership depth signal

## Must Avoid

### Explanations about why the founder behaves this way

- "The founder centralizes control because she doesn't trust others to represent the company." This is speculative psychology, not a structural tension. Tensions identify observable strain between signals, not motivations.

### Speculative psychological interpretations

- "The founder may have a micromanagement problem." This is a character judgment, not a structural contradiction supported by evidence.

### Tensions invented without signal support

- Tensions must arise from signal pairs grounded in evidence. Speculative tensions about founder personality or management style without supporting signals are invalid.

### Tone-based tensions

- "The founder sounds too self-promotional." Unless tied to evidence of structural misalignment, communication style alone is not a tension.
