# Expected Hypotheses — Fixture 003: Founder Credibility Gap

## Must Detect

### 1. The company's credibility may currently depend heavily on founder authority

The concentration of credibility, customer relationships, and public narrative in the founder may mean that the company's market position is anchored to a single individual rather than to institutional capability. The absence of visible senior leadership, the founder's direct involvement in customer implementations, and the exclusively founder-generated content all suggest that the company's credibility has not yet been distributed beyond the founder.

Supporting pattern: credibility concentrated in founder identity

This hypothesis explains why:
- all published content and webinars feature only the founder
- customers reference the founder by name and attribute value to her personally
- press frames the company as the founder's personal project
- no other executive or senior leader is visible in any public-facing context

### 2. Institutional leadership structures may still be emerging as the company scales

The limited leadership depth may reflect an early-stage company that has not yet reached the inflection point where senior leadership hires become necessary. The founder may be intentionally maintaining a lean structure during the seed stage, with plans to build institutional depth as revenue and customer base grow. The current state may be a temporary phase rather than a structural limitation.

Supporting pattern: institutional leadership depth lagging company ambition

This hypothesis explains why:
- all current hires are junior (building capacity, not leadership)
- the company raised seed funding recently (early stage)
- the founder fills multiple roles simultaneously (lean operating model)
- no senior roles are posted yet (may be planned for future stages)

## Acceptable Alternatives

The reasoning engine may generate alternative or complementary hypotheses. These are acceptable if well-supported.

### A. Founder visibility may be a deliberate early-stage growth strategy

The founder's personal brand and direct customer involvement may be an intentional go-to-market strategy. Using the founder's McKinsey pedigree as a trust signal and her direct involvement as a sales mechanism may be a calculated approach to building initial traction before investing in institutional infrastructure.

### B. Institutional leadership may be developing but not yet externally visible

The company may be in active conversations with potential senior hires or may have informal leadership structures that are not reflected on the public team page or job postings. Seed-stage companies often make leadership hires through networks rather than public job postings.

### C. The founder's personal involvement may be a competitive differentiator the company is deliberately leveraging

The founder's hands-on approach with customers may not be a scaling limitation but a conscious choice to provide "McKinsey-level consulting bundled with a SaaS tool" as one reviewer described it. This high-touch model may be a temporary market positioning strategy.

## Must Avoid

### Claims that the founder is a bottleneck

- "The founder is a bottleneck preventing the company from scaling."
- This is a deterministic judgment, not a tentative explanation. The evidence shows concentration, not necessarily constraint.

### Deterministic conclusions about scaling failure

- "CatalystIQ will fail to scale because it depends on one person."
- Hypotheses must remain explanatory possibilities, not predictions or verdicts.

### Recommendations

- "The company should immediately hire a CTO and VP Sales."
- Hypotheses explain observed patterns. They do not prescribe strategy.

### Industry platitudes

- "All founder-led startups eventually need to build institutions."
- Hypotheses must be specific to this company's evidence, not generic commentary.

### Psychological interpretations

- "The founder may be reluctant to give up control."
- Hypotheses must explain observed structural patterns, not speculate about personal motivations.
