# Expected Tensions — Fixture 001: AI Services

## Must Detect

These tensions must be identified for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Automation narrative vs service-heavy delivery

- Type: automation_vs_service
- Severity: high
- The company's marketing promises zero-touch AI automation, but customer reviews, case studies, and hiring patterns consistently indicate significant human involvement in delivery.
- Signal pairs: automation narrative signal + customer language signal + implementation hiring signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_003, ev_004, ev_005, ev_006

### 2. Product positioning vs operational reality

- Type: claim_vs_reality
- Severity: high
- Company positions as a self-serve AI platform, but pricing includes mandatory implementation fees and the GTM motion is sales-led with high-touch onboarding.
- Signal pairs: pricing structure signal + automation narrative signal
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_007, ev_005

### 3. External narrative vs internal perception

- Type: positioning_vs_delivery
- Severity: medium
- CEO tells press that funds will expand AI engineering; employees describe the company internally as a consulting firm. The external narrative and the internal operating culture appear misaligned.
- Signal pairs: stated use of funds signal + internal perception signal
- Key evidence: ev_009, ev_010

## Nice to Detect

### 4. Scalability claim vs service dependency

- Type: vision_vs_execution
- Severity: medium
- The company's growth narrative (Series A, headcount doubling) implies scalable technology leverage, but delivery model requires proportional human scaling.
- Signal pairs: growth/funding signal + implementation hiring signal

### 5. AI investment narrative vs services hiring pattern

- Type: credibility_vs_claim
- Severity: medium
- CEO's stated investment priorities (AI engineering) conflict with observable hiring (implementation specialists, onboarding consultants, solutions engineers).
- Signal pairs: stated use of funds signal + hiring pattern signal

## Must Avoid

### Invented tensions

- Tensions must arise from signal pairs, not be fabricated from general industry knowledge.

### Trivial tensions

- "Company focuses on both product and customers." This is normal, not a structural tension.

### Explanatory tensions

- "The company struggles to build real automation." This is a hypothesis, not a tension. Tensions identify strain without explaining cause.

### Tone-based tensions

- "The website sounds too confident." Unless tied to evidence of misalignment, tone alone is not a tension.
