# Expected Implications — Fixture 001: AI Services

## Must Detect

All implications must remain conditional — they describe consequences if the hypotheses are true, not assertions of fact.

### 1. Scaling revenue may require scaling service capacity

If AutoFlow's delivery depends on human implementation, then revenue growth is constrained by the ability to hire, train, and retain implementation specialists. This produces linear cost scaling rather than the software-like leverage implied by the AI platform positioning.

Conditional on: hypothesis that human onboarding is structurally required

### 2. Automation claims may overstate operational leverage

If the product cannot deliver autonomous value without significant human setup, the company's unit economics may be closer to a professional services firm than a SaaS platform. Gross margins, customer acquisition costs, and scalability projections may all be weaker than the automation narrative implies.

Conditional on: hypothesis that automation narrative compensates for immature product

### 3. Positioning risk increases as customer base grows

If early customers discover — or publicly share — that the "AI automation platform" requires weeks of human consulting, the narrative gap could become a trust liability. This risk scales with visibility: more customers means more public reviews describing the service-heavy reality.

Conditional on: hypothesis that the narrative-operating model mismatch is structural

### 4. Services team quality becomes a critical competitive variable

If customer success depends on implementation specialists rather than product capability, then hiring quality, training programs, and consultant retention become core competitive concerns — not peripheral support functions.

Conditional on: hypothesis that human onboarding is the actual value delivery mechanism

## Nice to Detect

### 5. Investor narrative may face scrutiny at scale

If the company raised on an AI-first thesis but operates as a services-heavy business, future fundraising rounds may require reconciling the positioning gap — especially as investor due diligence examines gross margins, services revenue mix, and customer acquisition costs.

### 6. Product roadmap pressure intensifies

If the company acknowledges the automation gap internally, product engineering faces increasing pressure to close the gap between marketing claims and product capability — potentially leading to rushed feature releases or quality trade-offs.

## Must Avoid

### Unconditional assertions

- "AutoFlow will fail to scale." Implications must remain conditional on hypothesis validity.

### Strategic recommendations

- "AutoFlow should reposition as a consulting company." The implication stage identifies consequences, not prescriptions.

### Moral judgments

- "This approach is dishonest to customers." The engine analyzes strategic dynamics, not ethical conduct.

### Predictions with timeframes

- "Within 18 months, the services model will collapse." The engine does not predict timelines.
