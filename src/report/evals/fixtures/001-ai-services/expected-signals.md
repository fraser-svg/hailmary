# Expected Signals — Fixture 001: AI Services

## Must Detect

These signals must be extracted for the fixture to pass.

### 1. Automation narrative stronger than delivery automation

- Category: positioning
- Inference: light_inference
- The company's marketing emphasizes zero-touch AI automation, but customer evidence describes significant human involvement in setup and configuration.
- Key evidence: ev_001, ev_002, ev_003, ev_004

### 2. Customer language credits human support, not AI

- Category: customer
- Inference: direct
- Across multiple review platforms, customers attribute value to onboarding specialists and consultants rather than to autonomous product capability.
- Key evidence: ev_003, ev_004

### 3. Implementation hiring signals disproportionate to company size

- Category: talent
- Inference: direct
- 13 open services-oriented roles at a 110-person company (12% headcount growth in services alone). Hiring skews heavily toward implementation, not engineering.
- Key evidence: ev_005, ev_008

### 4. Pricing structure includes mandatory service fees

- Category: pricing
- Inference: direct
- Onboarding fees of $5,000-$25,000 are mandatory on Growth and Enterprise tiers, indicating setup is not self-serve despite automation positioning.
- Key evidence: ev_007

### 5. Case study reveals consulting engagement model

- Category: operations
- Inference: light_inference
- Company-published case study describes a 12-week consulting engagement with weekly strategy sessions and custom sequence building — a service model, not a product model.
- Key evidence: ev_006

## Nice to Detect

These signals add depth but are not required for passing.

### 6. Internal perception diverges from external narrative

- Category: credibility
- Inference: strong_inference
- Employee review describes the company as "a consulting company that happens to have a software product," contradicting the AI-first external positioning.
- Key evidence: ev_010

### 7. Stated use of funds contradicts hiring pattern

- Category: operations
- Inference: light_inference
- CEO states Series A funds will go toward "doubling the AI engineering team," but observable hiring is concentrated in implementation and services.
- Key evidence: ev_009, ev_005, ev_008

### 8. Professional services as revenue stream

- Category: pricing
- Inference: light_inference
- Implementation fees are substantial relative to subscription pricing, suggesting services are a meaningful revenue component rather than a cost center.
- Key evidence: ev_007

## Must Avoid

The signal extraction stage must NOT produce these.

### Restatements of dossier facts

- "The company offers an AI sales automation platform."
- This is a fact, not an analytical observation.

### Strategic conclusions

- "AutoFlow is actually a services company, not a SaaS company."
- This is a hypothesis, not a signal. Signals observe; they do not conclude.

### Cosmetic observations

- "The company website uses modern design and AI imagery."
- Not strategically relevant unless tied to a narrative mismatch.

### Moral judgments

- "The company is misleading customers about its AI capabilities."
- Signals must remain observational and neutral.
